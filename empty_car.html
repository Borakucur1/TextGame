Text Me When You're Home

You step through the sliding door into the next car.

It is quieter immediately.

Too quiet.

The seats are empty except for a forgotten receipt, a crushed can rolling slightly with every turn, and a dark jacket balled up in the corner.

The lights flicker once, then settle into a tired buzz.

Your phone loses service.

For a second, you tell yourself this is better. More space. Less people. Less weirdness.

Then the door behind you slides shut.

The train dips into a tunnel, and your reflection appears in the black window beside you.

You suddenly miss being near other people.

  • Go back to the first car
  • Get off at the next stop
  • Stay in the empty car
