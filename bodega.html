Text Me When You're Home

You spot the bodega before you realize how badly you needed it.

Fluorescent lights. Lotto signs. Chips stacked too high. A fridge humming like it has been working the night shift for twenty years.

The bell above the door rings when you step inside.

The cashier looks up for half a second, then back down at their phone.

Normal. Bright. Public.

You grab a water from the fridge just to have a reason to stay inside.

Outside, the street still looks wet and empty through the glass.

Your phone buzzes.

“Stay somewhere bright. Call me.”

You take one slow breath.

For the first time in a while, you feel like you can think.

  • Call your friend from inside
  • Ask if there is somewhere safe nearby to wait
  • Leave and keep walking the main road
