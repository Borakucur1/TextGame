Text Me When You're Home

You recognize the block before you fully believe it.

The corner store. The cracked sidewalk. The streetlight that always flickers like it has something personal against you.

Home is close enough to see now.

Your friend is still on the phone, their voice small and tinny against your ear.

“I’m serious. Don’t hang up until you’re inside.”

You laugh, but your hand tightens around your keys anyway.

The last block feels longer than every block before it.

Your phone drops to 2%.

The front door is right there.

  • Send “almost home” and go inside
  • Stay on the call until the door locks behind you
  • Ignore your phone and rush the last block
