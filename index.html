Text Me When You're Home

The club door swings shut behind you and the bass keeps thumping in your ribs like it wants to come home with you. Ears are ringing.

You definitely had one too many drinks. Not “I forgot my name” drunk more like “I think I can navigate my way home, but I kinda see double” drunk.

Your friends do the end of night breakup montage: hugs, loud promises, “I'm starving” “I'm calling an Uber.”

Someone yells, “TEXT ME WHEN YOU'RE HOME!” like it's a sacred ritual. Then the group dissolves into different directions.

Your phone buzzes again. Text me when you're home. You made your way to the train station and stare at the station entrance. You are unfortunately the main character now.
