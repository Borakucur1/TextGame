Text Me When You're Home

The platform smells like metal and brake dust. The air is colder down here.

The sign says the next train is delayed. Of course it is.

Your phone buzzes again: text me when you're home.

A train screams through the opposite track without stopping. Wind whips your jacket for half a second before the tunnel goes quiet again.

Somewhere farther down the platform, someone laughs too loudly. Another person is asleep across three seats.

You check the time again. Later than you thought.

This is the moment you either check in… or you don't.

  • Share your location + text “on my way”
  • Don't text anyone (you just want to get home)
  • Ask the booth/security what's going on
