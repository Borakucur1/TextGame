Text Me When You're Home

You get off at the next stop because it feels like the right thing to do.

Then the doors close behind you.

The train pulls away, and the platform goes quiet in a way that feels personal.

You look up at the station sign.

Wrong stop.

Not one stop wrong. Not “I can fix this easily” wrong.

The kind of wrong where the map suddenly looks like a puzzle made by someone who hates you.

You climb the stairs to street level.

The air outside is colder. The stores are closed. A traffic light changes for nobody.

Your phone buzzes.

“Where are you? Your dot stopped moving.”

You check your battery.

5%.

You need to make the next choice carefully.

  • Call your friend before the phone dies
  • Walk toward the nearest bright store
  • Follow the GPS shortcut
