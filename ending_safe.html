Text Me When You're Home

You made it.

Your keys slip twice before you finally get the door open. The lock clicks behind you and the entire night seems to stop breathing at once.

Silence.

Shoes off. Phone charger. A glass of water you almost spill because your hands are still slightly shaking from adrenaline, alcohol, or both.

The city is still moving somewhere outside your window, but it no longer feels responsible for you.

You sit on the edge of your bed for a second longer than necessary, replaying every tiny decision that got you here.

The train. The strangers. The dark blocks. The moments you almost convinced yourself nothing bad could happen.

Your phone lights up in your hand.

You text:

home.

Three dots appear instantly.

“THANK GOD.”

“next time im literally forcing you into an uber.”

You laugh for the first time all night.

It's a small word. Four letters.

But tonight, it means everything.
