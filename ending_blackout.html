Text Me When You're Home

Your phone vibrates one last time.

1%.

You try to unlock it too quickly. The brightness burns your eyes. For half a second, your reflection stares back at you through fingerprints and cracks.

No new messages. No new trains. Just the sound of distant traffic and your own footsteps trying not to sound nervous.

You finally type it:

home soon

The message spins. Sending...

Then the screen dies.

No map. No battery. No tiny blue dot for someone to watch move through the city.

The street suddenly feels bigger than it did five seconds ago.

A car slows down near the curb.

You look up.

The city keeps moving like nothing happened.

And somewhere far away, a phone waits for a text that never comes.
